Why CFS Exists

Markets are becoming a data problem. We think finance education should catch up.

Computational Finance Society was built to create a space where finance is explored through analytical thinking, quantitative methods, and technology — not just memorized definitions.

As financial markets grow more data-driven, understanding mathematics, programming, and computational techniques matters more than a textbook chapter ever could. CFS exists to make those ideas approachable, hands-on, and genuinely useful — for students, self-starters, and future founders alike.

Data over guessworkEvery concept is tied back to a model, a formula, or a dataset you can actually test.
Built for beginners, sharpened for buildersWhether you're opening a spreadsheet for the first time or shipping your own model, there's a level here for you.
Free, and staying that wayNo paywalls on the core content. CFS is run independently, by a teenager, for teenagers.

FAQ

Quick answers.

The questions that land in the inbox most — answered once, here, so you don't have to wait on an email.

Is CFS actually free?

Yes — every research area, project write-up, and Finance Lab calculator is free, with no account or paywall. That's a deliberate, permanent choice, not a limited-time thing.

Who is this actually for?

Teenagers first — curious students, self-starters, and young entrepreneurs — but anyone new to quantitative finance will find it useful. No prior finance background is assumed.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. The Finance Lab calculators need zero coding. Some Research Areas and Projects go deeper into data and computation for anyone who wants to push further.

Is anything here financial advice?

No. CFS is an educational project. Calculators and content are for learning, not personalized investment, tax, or financial advice — always double-check important decisions with a qualified professional.

How often is content updated?

The Desk Note near the top of the homepage is updated directly through the admin panel, often without any code changes — so check back regularly for what's new.

Can I suggest a project or contribute an idea?

Always. Head to the Contact section and reach out by email, Instagram, or Discord — project suggestions are exactly what the "Have a project idea?" card is there for.
